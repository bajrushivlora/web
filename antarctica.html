Antarctica Survival Guide: Print

Print
Editorial

Have you ever wondered what life is like on the coldest, driest continent on earth ? I compiled everything you need to know about visiting Antarctica into a small handbook: the Antarctica Survival Guide. I researched extensively through established databases, magazines, movies, and online forums. I was able to pull concrete information about climate, travel routes, restrictions, rules, and regulations from the established sources. However, I was most fascinated by the information I found through personal anecdotes on Quora and Reddit’s r/antarctica thread.

The survival guide craves a step-by-step structure to tame all of the information-rich content. The organization of the book closely mimics the real world process of visiting the continent. The key to survival in Antarctica is preparedness in all facets, from travel visas to personal hygiene. I split the guide into two digestible parts— the preparation, and the arrival. The result is an accessible, pocket-sized guide that begins with a foundation of standard content from established resources, and topped with the conglomerate of personal experiences that are scattered throughout the crevices of the internet.

Antarctica Survival Guide closely resembles the size and feel of a passport book. The guide is printed on matte black paper, topped with electric blue and green text and graphics. The monospace typeface is Antarctican Mono. These design elements work to create a legible guide that feels scientific and reliable. The refined stylistic elements create space for the guide’s content to shine. The reader can simultaneously familiarize themselves with the important standards and expectations of Antarctica, and enjoy the sprinkles of content that reveal the quizzical nature of life on the continent.

The printed guide has a sister digital guide: visit https://antarctica-guide.com.
