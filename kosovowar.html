Kosovo War: Print

Print
Editorial

In the late 1990s, the people of Kosovo survived a two-year long war of terror, genocide, and persecution. The ethnic Kosovo-Albanians lived in oppression by the Serbian regime and its people. There is a deep rooted tension between the two countries. A former Serbian province, Kosovo has fought to be recognized as an independent territory— an act that Serbia does not recognize nor support. In an attempt to rid the country of Serbia’s rules and oppression, the people of Kosovo became political activists and demonstrators for their country. The rebellion sparked controversy, violence, and fierce reaction from Serbia— war was officially declared.

The content of the Kosovo War book is taken entirely from its respective Wikipedia page: Kosovo War. In this project, I picked a Wikipedia article with an appropriate text length to be re-contextualized into a book. Using the entirety of the Kosovo War Wikipedia content, I organized the sections, subsections, and footnotes appropriately. No images were used in this book.

Accompanying the text is a smaller narrative in the point-of-view of my aunt, who lived through the war as a teenager. She would send me voice memos, telling the story of how they fled Kosovo. The narrative subtly pulls the reader away from the rich, textbook content of the Wikipedia article, travelling back in time with my aunt in 1999. The text from Wikipedia and the story from my aunt provide two parallel timelines of the war.

The text is typeset in Courier, a monospace that mimics the official nature and documentation of this time in history. The narrative is typeset in all capital letters in ITC Avant Garde, a quiet sans-serif that feels minimal and slender, an ode to the sensitive, fragile time of the war. Each chapter represents a shade of grey, progressing from the white book cover to the black back cover. Each shade ties together with the book’s front header-“War is Not”, and the book’s back header- “Black and White”. Every war has its own history and future, and everything in between. This book is one interpretation of the war, and there are many more lost voices that have their story to tell.
