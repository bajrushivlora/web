Synesthesia Catalog

Print
Editorial

Math is red, Science is green, English is blue, and French is purple. Have you experienced a synesthetic perception ? Chances are, you’ve experienced synesthesia to a certain degree. It’s a phenomenon where one or more sensory perceptions produce unrelated sensory perceptions. It’s like tasting a word, or smelling a color. These two sensory perceptions are unrelated, but we automatically experience that association for one reason or another. I find that I experience synesthesia with letters and words, a branch of synesthesia known as Ordinal Linguistic Personification. OLP is an umbrella term for synesthetic perceptions where one may assign colors, genders, and personalities to numbers, letters, and words. To me, every letter in the alphabet has a defined gender, personality, and attitude.

So, I decided to put this form of synesthesia to good use when I was presented with a project prompt. The challenge ?— create a catalog that re-contextualizes a set of objects, products, or ideas. So, I created a catalog of every letter in the alphabet, and placed them in the context of personification through my synesthesia. Each letter has an assigned gender, personality trait(s), and a real-life photo spread of who I imagine this letter would look like if it were an actual person. With these 26 letters, I then grouped them into 5 personality categories for the purpose of the assignment: “weird” letters, “bold” letters, “woke” letters, “honest” letters, and “chaotic” letters. I also added a drop quote, an ode to Instagram's bio feature.

The final book is a whopping 1.5 inches thick, and is printed on waffle-textured paper. I typeset all text in Helvetica. The clean sans-serif standardizes all the text, allowing for the images, colors, and textures to dominate. At the end of the catalog, you will find an index for colors, and an index of key personality traits.
